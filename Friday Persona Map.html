Task 2: Stakeholder Persona and Journey Mapping for Improvement Opportunities

The persona for whom we are developing our customer journey is detailed in the persona map illustrated in Table 2. After developing a detailed explanation of the character that we have built to be the individual customer, we specified the most relevant features that we thought could help us identify her pain points through the Empathy Map illustrated in Figure 9, in Appendix C.

Table 2: Persona Map

Persona Map Section Details
Fictional Name and Picture Lydia
Demographics Gender: Female
Age: 23
Ethnicity: Biracial (Anglo-Saxon and Indian)
Marital Status: Single
Educational Level: Tertiary Education
Occupation: Operations Coordinator, Team Member
Income Level: $70K
Location: Urban
Background Lydia experienced mental health challenges in a high socio-economic background.
The only child, no siblings, very attached to parents. Parents are the most loving and supportive, always receiving “princess treatment.” Just completed a bachelor's degree and has directly transitioned into a master's program with no work experience. Left family to move to Australia for pursuing education and dream of being independent.
She is also navigating the workplace for the first time, learning how to communicate with diverse colleagues. She seeks practical guidance to help her reach a fulfilling career path.
Goals and Motivation To become independent and self-satisfied after a life of being supported and cared for by her family.
Wants to prove that she can stand on her own and achieve her dreams without relying on their direct support.
Have a family of her own.
Run a female-founded business that integrates technology to help companies grow internally and externally.
Challenges and Pain Points Self-discovery and finding courses for practical skills.
Difficulty in communicating her skills in a business context, as tertiary education hasn’t prepared her for this.
Constantly missing her family and feeling the pain of being away from them, especially since they are her greatest strength.
Lack of sleep and exhaustion from juggling the pressures of higher education and adapting to a new country and culture.
Managing life independently for the first time after a long period of support is a key challenge. Balancing studies, emotional well-being, and practical responsibilities is difficult.
Interests and Behaviours Playing with her cat
Swimming and going to the gym
Drawing, which is a form of emotional release and a distraction from overthinking.
Socializing with friends
Watching business leadership podcasts
Learning new skills for personal and career growth.
Technology Usage Frequent tech user at an amateur to intermediate level.
Needs Needs guidance to navigate her career path, seeking practical skills that tertiary education may not have provided.
Environment/Context of Use Uses technology primarily at home or at work.
Personality Hopeful, yet confused and naive
Ethically inclined and curious
Sweet, gentle, understanding, and always smiling. Known for being nice and approachable, with a warm, friendly personality.
Enjoys sharing ideas and being funny.
Quotes "I have a question."
"I understand there is a longer-term plan, but how can we improve the current situation?"
"How do I get to the point?"
"What do you think?"
Displays resilience by stepping out of her comfort zone despite emotional challenges.
